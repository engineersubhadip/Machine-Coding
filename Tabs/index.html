About

Michelangelo Buonarroti, born in 1475, was a true polymath of the Renaissance era. Renowned as a sculptor, painter, architect, and poet, Michelangelo left an indelible mark on Western art. His masterpieces include the iconic statue of David, the breathtaking frescoes of the Sistine Chapel ceiling in Vatican City, and the architectural design of St. Peter's Basilica. Michelangelo's work is characterized by a profound sense of human anatomy, emotional intensity, and a devotion to classical ideals. His contributions to the arts have solidified him as one of the greatest artists in history.
